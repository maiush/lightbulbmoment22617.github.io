Hey, I’m Sharan. I’m currently a PhD candidate at the University of Cambridge through the AI4ER CDT. For the past one and a half years I was a research assistant at the University of Edinburgh, where I looked at the health effects of exposure to air pollution. Practically this involved a lot of work with causal discovery algorithms and targeted causal learning. My current broad research-goals are to build AI-based tools to accelerate science, specially in the domains of the physical sciences and environmental risk. I'm interested in representation learning, interpretability and language models. I also think AI Safety is extremely important, whether that means near-term issues of AI Ethics, AI Governance, or future existential threats from AGI/ASI.

I have a BSc in cs + maths from Edinburgh and an MSc in stats from Imperial College London. Both my Bachelor’s and Master’s theses were on causal discovery with time series – the former an application and the latter on hyperparameter tuning and benchmarking.

I spend a lot of time thinking about meditation, effective donating, whatever movie I watched yesterday, and how much I miss Scottish tap water, so definitely reach out if you want to talk about any of those (or perhaps about research, I guess). You can do that by email or through the links below.
