Hey, I’m Sharan. I’m currently a research assistant at the University of Edinburgh, where I look at the health effects of exposure to air pollution. Practically this involves a lot of stats and a lot of machine learning because I’m interested in using these tools for problems in epidemiology and environmental risk. I’m very interested in any problem at the intersection of ML and causal inference, whether that’s the use of causal information for meta-learning, explainability or robustness, or the use of machine learning for causal discovery and effect estimation in observational data. I also think AI Safety is extremely important, and I keep it in mind when it comes to any work I do and any work I plan on doing in the future.

My BSc was in CompSci + Maths at Edinburgh and my MSc was in Statistics at Imperial College London. Both my Bachelor’s and Master’s theses were on causal discovery with time series – the former an application and the latter on hyperparameter tuning and benchmarking. In autumn 2023 I'll be moving to Cambridge to begin a PhD, as part of the AI for Environmental Risks CDT.

I spend a lot of time thinking about meditation (insight/metta is a big part of my daily life), effective altruism, whatever movie I watched yesterday, and AI Safety work I can get stuck into this summer before my PhD, so please reach out if you want to talk about any of those.
